Common Types of Scams in Australia

    • Investment Scams: These scams offer fake money-making opportunities to prey on the victim's enthusiasm and steal their cash.


    • Dating & Romance Scams: Scammers use dating or friendship platforms to manipulate and convince individuals to give them money, leaving victims both broke and broken-hearted.


    • False Billing: Victims are falsely invoiced or charged for services they did not request or receive.


    • Phishing: These scams involve attempts to trick individuals into providing sensitive data like passwords or credit card numbers.


    • Jobs & Employment Scams: Scammers offer a ‘guaranteed’ way to make fast money or a high-paying job for little effort to trick individuals into handing over their money.


    • Remote Access Scams: Scammers trick individuals into granting them access to their computers, potentially stealing data or injecting malware.


    • Threats to Life, Arrest or Other: These scams involve threats to the victim's life or well-being to extort money or personal information.


    • Identity Theft: These scams involve using someone else's identity to steal money or gain other benefits.


    • Rebate Scams: Victims are lured by the promise of a rebate or refund, often for taxes or other expenses, only to be defrauded.


    • Classified Scams: These scams target individuals selling items or properties on classified ads platforms by posing as genuine buyers or sellers.

The Importance of Passkeys

What are passkeys?

Passkeys are a more secure and convenient way to access your accounts and devices without traditional passwords. These passkeys are unique to you and can come in various forms, such as:


  • Biometrics: Your fingerprint, face scan, or even your voice can serve as a passkey. Devices like smartphones and laptops use these biometric features to verify your identity.

  • Hardware Tokens: Some systems provide physical hardware tokens, like USB keys or smart cards, that you plug into your device to prove your identity.

  • One-Time Codes: Passkeys can also be generated as one-time codes and sent to your trusted device, which you enter to access your account.

  • Email Magic Links: Instead of entering a password, you receive an email link that, when clicked, serves as your passkey to log in.
