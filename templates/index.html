Types of Scams

Romance Scams

Romance scams involve individuals using fake personas to build emotional connections with victims, often online. These scammers exploit trust to extract money or personal information, preying on victims' emotions. They fabricate stories, establish rapport, and then request financial aid for various reasons.

Find Out More

Investment Scams

Investment scams are tricks that make people put money into something, but they end up losing it. These scams promise big profits easily, which sounds nice, but it's not true. Scammers use lies to get people interested, saying things like they have special deals or know secret things. They might even make fake investment companies. At first, they might give a bit of money back to seem real, but then everything falls apart, and people lose a lot. To stay safe, learn a lot before investing, ask experts for advice, and be careful if someone you don't know offers you a deal that seems too good.

Find Out More

Text or SMS Scams

Text or SMS scams are when people send fake messages to your phone to steal your information or money. They might pretend to be important companies and ask you to give them your private details or send them money. They use tricky words to make you feel rushed or excited. To stay safe, be careful with messages from numbers you don't know, don't click on strange links, and never share personal stuff or money through texts unless you're sure it's real.

Find Out More

Phone Scams

Phone call scams are when some individuals make fake phone calls to try to trick you into giving them money or personal information. These scammers might pretend to be from a bank, government agency, or a company you trust. They use tricky tactics like telling you that something is wrong with your account or offering prizes to get you to act fast. They can sound very convincing. To stay safe, be cautious when getting calls from unknown numbers, never give out your private details or send money over the phone, and hang up if something feels off.

Find Out More

Email Scams

Last year, Australians lost a lot of money, $77 million, because of email scams. Tricky people sent emails that looked really important, pretending to be from the government, police, or businesses. They made the emails seem real by using the same logos and almost the same email addresses as the real places. These scammers can even copy an email address to make their fake emails look real. It's important to be careful with emails, even if they look real, and not give them your money or information. That way, you can keep yourself and your money safe.
